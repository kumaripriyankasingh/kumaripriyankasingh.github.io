# The "Happily Ever After" Delusion

**Disclaimer:** This post might not sit well with everyone. Truth is like a strong cup of black coffee—bitter, harsh, and likely to keep you up at night. 
If you prefer your reality sugar-coated, look away now. 

Still here? 

Grab a pinch of salt. You’re going to need it.

---

### **The "Settle Down" Starter Pack**

In our society, a boy and a girl get married because, well, that’s just what humans do, right? It’s the ultimate life quest. We’ve been convinced that marriage is a magical vending machine: you insert a ring, and out comes emotional support, financial stability, and a boost to your social status.

From a legal standpoint, it’s basically a fancy way to ensure your stuff goes to your spouse instead of a random cousin you haven't spoken to since 1998. Indian law practically insists on it. Plus, let’s not forget the parents. For them, marriage is just a project management task: "Project Kid" is finally settled, the lineage is secure, and the neighbors can finally stop asking questions.

Marriage: because "getting settled" sounds much nicer than "signing a lifelong liability waiver."

---

### **The Routine vs. The Blunder**

So, the deed is done. If you’re lucky, you actually like the person. For a while, it’s all excitement and fulfilling each other’s needs—until the "new car smell" wears off and becomes a "tuesday night laundry" routine.

But what if you aren’t lucky? What if you realize you’ve tethered yourself to someone who is only there to get their own needs met? That’s when the "blunder" realization hits. Those with courage might try to escape the burning building; those without usually just sit in the smoke and call it "destiny."

---


### **The Live Toy Strategy**

Once the marriage starts feeling a bit... quiet (or loud for the wrong reasons), the next logical step appears: The Child.

It’s the ultimate validation. "Look! We produced something together!" A baby is essentially a live toy for the family—an adorable, screaming distraction that allows you to ignore your boring partner. We tell ourselves these kids will achieve the dreams we gave up on, carry our names into a future where no one even remembers our great-grandfathers, and be our retirement plan.

Suddenly, your entire universe shrinks to the size of one small human. You’d walk through fire for them—often at the cost of your friends, your own parents, and your sanity. You aren't just a parent; you're a martyr for a legacy that most people will forget in two generations.

---


### **The Full Circle**

Eventually, the "live toy" grows up. They stop being cute, start having opinions, and—if you’ve conditioned them well—they decide that marriage is the "logical next step" for them, too.

Meanwhile, the parents grow old and realize they are back where they started: stuck with the same partner they’ve been ignoring for thirty years. But now, they’re too tired to leave and too broke to start over. So, they live out a "miserable ever after," not by choice, but because they’ve run out of options.

---

### **The Bitter Truth**
We are told marriage brings forever joy, but often it’s just a chain reaction of solving one problem by creating a bigger one.

We think marriage is the container for love, but here’s the kicker: marriage binds, and love never binds. If you think a spouse or a kid is the solution to your internal void, you’re doing a massive disservice to yourself and the world.

No one is actually "supposed" to fulfill your needs. That’s an inside job. If you’re kind, shower some love on others—but don't expect a legal contract or a biological legacy to do the heavy lifting for your soul.

The Moral? Don't get married because it's "logical." Logic is for spreadsheets; life is for living.
